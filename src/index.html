About-Petra

The history of Petra is long, its valley was highly valued for its easy defense. However, its first inhabitants were nomads , there are no physical traces of the homes other than from the Nabataean era , since this civilization built and lived there for a long time. After the Byzantine period, the place is practically abandoned, so there are few sources that evoke that time, making it difficult to reconstruct the history of the city over a long period. After the rediscovery of the city by Jean Louis Burckhardt in 1812, many sources evoking the history of Petra have been found in ancient writings.

How to reach

Petra is located halfway between the Gulf of Aqaba and the Dead Sea at an altitude of 800 to 1,396 m asl (meters above sea level) in a valley in the mountainous region of Edom , east of the Arabah Valley . Today, Petra is around 200 km (kilometres) southwest of the Jordanian capital Amman , approximately 3 hours by car.
